How do I fund my studies abroad?

Studying abroad can be an expensive affair and although costs vary from institute to institute and country to country, many students seek some kind of financial aid to support their studies overseas. At any given university for the undergraduate level, you may land up with a bill of over $250,000 – at the end of four years. If you are thinking of pursuing an MBA from a top institute, you may land up paying $200,000 for a two-year program. To remedy this, there are institutes that offer need-based financial aid to international students and several independent organizations that provide merit-based scholarships and loans. Combination of need-based financial aid, scholarships and loans may in some cases, pay for your entire education abroad.

FAQs

When should I apply for scholarships?

As university applications have deadlines, scholarships have deadlines too. It is important to research the requirements and apply before the deadline and the earlier you apply, the more number of scholarships are available.

When should I apply for loans?

Students can apply for education loans after receiving the offer letter from universities. However, as the time duration is less between securing admission and payment of fees, there are banks that allow students to apply for educational loans even before securing admission.

Do you have any tie-ups with universities?

We do not have tie-ups with any universities as it would hinder us from being independent and unbiased counsellors.

Can you guarantee me a scholarship?

No, we cannot guarantee you scholarships. Your likelihood of getting a scholarship depends on the respective university, your profile and the amount of scholarship you have applied for.

What kinds of students get scholarships?

The most common factor for achieving scholarship is your grades and standardized test scores. However universities also look over the internships, volunteering, project work, leadership activities or any other extra curricular activities that will help your case.

Is there a chance of getting a 100% scholarship?

Yes, students with an excellent profile and top-most standardized test scores are capable of getting a 100% scholarship.

Is there a different application for scholarships or is it the university application only?

This depends on the scholarship you plan to apply for. Universities have many scholarships of their own which you can either apply to when you are applying for a course or you separately apply for later. If you are applying for a scholarship from a separate organization or your government, they generally have a different application process.

What is the difference between need-based and merit-based financial aid?

Need-based financial aid is for students who will not be able to afford their education and living expenses at the university and thus need financial aid.

Merit-based financial aid is for students who have an exceptional profile and good academic background.

What should I do if I don’t get a scholarship?

If you don’t manage to get a scholarship, you have several other options open for you. You can apply for a loan or you can apply for a graduate assistantship. Assistantships come in the form of teaching and research assistantships and you are generally given monthly stipends to take care of your needs in exchange for working with the university faculty for a few hours every week.
