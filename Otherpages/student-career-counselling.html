What we can help within career counselling

How to choose a career? It’s a question students have been asking themselves for generations. Some are lucky enough to know exactly what they want in life and can plan and make appropriate choices – it’s as if their career chose them. Most of us are not so fortunate, and the enormous range of career choices and the even bigger range of academic choices can leave us feeling somewhat lost and confused.

As a professional education consultant and career counsellor I can help you to find your right path in life.

Know your strengths

A key part of the process is to get to know you as an individual. And for you to start to get to know yourself a little better. In this way, we can discover your strengths, talents, and skills, but we can also take an honest look at your areas for development and find out what doesn’t suit you.

As an MBTI Practitioner, I will take you through some of the world’s most respected personality and aptitude tests, to provide you with objective insights into your public and private personas and your true motivators.

I limit the number of students that I take on so that I can give everyone my attention. To give the best advice you need to trust me, and I need to know you – I believe the only way to achieve those goals is by dealing with my students one-to-one.

Map your journey

The key to effective career counselling is to help you draw up your career map. Note I didn’t say “give you a map”. My role is to guide, to counsel, perhaps to cajole, but you must make the informed decisions for yourself and to take ownership of your study and career journey. Once you’ve made those decisions I can help with applications, mock interviews, advice on personal statements and resumes, and support you wherever we’ve identified it’s required.

FAQs

What will you help me with within a career counselling session?

In the initial counselling session, we will conduct a basic psychometric test to discover your likes and dislikes. We will be evaluating your profile, guiding you through a career mapping plan, and profile-building strategies. Additionally, we will be providing you with preliminary Information about Universities and Courses in India and abroad considering your specific preferences.

Can you tell me which university I should apply to?

In the initial counselling session, we will provide you with a brief overview of the universities that are suitable for your profile. However, a detailed list of universities will be provided after you enrol for our full study abroad counselling packages after in-depth research for your specific profile.

Do I need to take a psychometric test? Who are these types of tests made for?

We recommend that you give the basic career survey test that is a part of the initial counselling session first. This test will assist us in providing you with the career options that you could consider. Alternatively, you could go in for the in-depth psychometric tests namely MBTI, Strong, and DAT. These tests will help you understand your personality, what comes naturally to you, and what your strengths and weaknesses are. 

What is included in career planning or mapping?

Based on your profile and the basic career survey test, Dr. Gupta will suggest career path options that you should follow. This includes the different ways in which you can enhance your profile e.g. the extracurricular activities that you could undertake, the ideal time to start your preparation, the standardized tests you need to give, the future career prospects of different fields, etc. Adopting a holistic approach, Dr. Gupta will mentor you to achieve your dreams.

When is the ideal time to start career counselling?

The earlier, the better. Anyone who is sincerely considering any career prospect can find Dr. Gupta’s advice insightful. Students from the 6th grade onwards can go in for our Student Mentorship Program. The purpose of our program is to enhance the existing knowledge and skills of students from a young age. Through this program, students will gain insight into Career Guidance, Profile Building, Skill Development, Mindfulness & Wellness, and Psychometric Testing. Dr. Gupta’s mentorship assists students to gain clarity and focus in all aspects of their lives enabling them to make informed decisions. Alternatively, students can explore career counselling from the 10th or 11th grade.
